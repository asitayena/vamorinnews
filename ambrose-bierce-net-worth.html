Ambrose Bierce Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born in 1842 in a log cabin at Horse Cave Creek, Ohio, Bierce’s upbringing was modest yet steeped in intellectual stimulation.

From the age of 15, after he left home to become a printer’s devil for an abolitionist newspaper, his journey into the realms of literature and journalism began.

This role, although not lucrative, was pivotal in shaping his future career, laying the foundation for his development as a writer and journalist.

Military Influence and Literary Ascent

His participation in the American Civil War profoundly influenced his writing and ideological stance.

His war experiences not only provided material for his future works but also introduced him to the harsh realities of life and death, which he explored through his satirical and often cynical viewpoint.

His notable works during this period, such as Tales of Soldiers and Civilians, reflect his deep understanding of the human condition and the absurdities of existence, which garnered critical acclaim and increased his prominence in literary circles.

Journalism and Major Works

Post-war, Bierce settled into a career in journalism, becoming one of the most influential journalists in the United States.

His sharp wit and incisive satire were evident in his columns and essays, particularly in his work with the San Francisco Examiner, owned by William Randolph Hearst.

His literary output included a wide range of genres, prominently satire through which he critiqued society and human nature.

The Devil’s Dictionary, one of his most celebrated works, exemplifies his unique style of defining common terms in bitterly humorous ways.

This book, along with his short stories like An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge, not only solidified his reputation but would have contributed to any potential earnings from his writing.

Personal Life

His personal life was marked by both tragedy and controversy.

He married Mary Ellen Mollie Day in 1871, and they had three children, though both of his sons met untimely deaths—one from suicide and the other from pneumonia exacerbated by alcoholism.

The loss of his children and a scandalous separation from Mollie in 1888, after he discovered her letters from another man, led to their divorce in 1904.

An avowed agnostic, Bierce was also plagued by health issues, including lifelong asthma and complications from war wounds, which contributed to episodes of fainting and irritability.

Influence and Disappearance

His influence extended beyond his lifetime, affecting the development of American literature and journalism.

His disappearance in Mexico in 1914 while covering the Mexican Revolution added a layer of enduring mystery and intrigue to his legacy.

FAQs about Ambrose Bierce

Who was Ambrose Bierce?

He was an American author and journalist noted for his cunning and sardonic view of human nature. He was active in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

What is Ambrose Bierce famous for?

He is famous for his mysterious disappearance in 1913 and for his works such as An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge and The Devil’s Dictionary, which display his mastery of the horror genre and satire.

When and where was Ambrose Bierce born?

He was born on June 24, 1842, in Meigs County, Ohio, USA.

What are some of his notable works?

Some of his notable works include Tales of Soldiers and Civilians, Can Such Things Be? and a collection of biting definitions published as The Cynic’s Word Book which later became The Devil’s Dictionary.

Did he fight in the Civil War?

Yes, he served as a Union officer in the Civil War. His experiences during the war profoundly influenced much of his later writing, particularly his short stories about the war.

How did Ambrose Bierce die?

The exact circumstances of his death remain a mystery. He disappeared in 1913 after traveling to Mexico to witness the Mexican Revolution. He was never seen again, and his fate is one of the great mysteries of American literature.

What was The Devil’s Dictionary?

The Devil’s Dictionary is a satirical dictionary written by Ambrose Bierce. It offers reinterpretations of English words with cynicism and dark humor.

What style is he known for?

Bierce is known for his biting wit, cynical disposition, and a clear, unsentimental style that often incorporated grim realism.

Are there any films or adaptations of Bierce’s works?

Yes, several of Bierce’s stories have been adapted into films, including multiple versions of An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge and adaptations of his other stories into various television and film formats.

What influenced his writing?

Bierce’s writing was heavily influenced by his experiences in the Civil War, which often reflected the brutality and futility of conflict. His cynical view of human nature also shaped his literary style.
